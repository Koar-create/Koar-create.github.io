Starting November 2025, this research targets urban wind field reconstruction at the building-resolving scale. By monodirectionally coupling the **WRF mesoscale model** with the **OpenFOAM CFD solver**, we developed a downscaling pipeline capable of resolving complex urban aerodynamics (e.g., building wakes and street canyons).

## Validation Results

The framework was validated against multi-station **LiDAR vertical wind profiles** in the Guangzhou Central Business District (CBD). Statistical results across three valid time steps (0000, 0400, 0800 UTC) show a positive **Skill Score (SS > 0)**, demonstrating that the CFD model significantly reduces wind speed biases in the lower urban boundary layer compared to the standalone WRF model.

*Note: The 1200 UTC case was excluded as the stable atmospheric boundary layer suppressed Turbulence Kinetic Energy (TKE), rendering steady-state RANS simulations inapplicable.*

## Future Directions

Upcoming work includes the implementation of a **split-roughness boundary strategy** to eliminate double-counting artifacts in ground roughness, and the transition from RANS to **LES/WMLES** for resolving transient gust features and non-Gaussian turbulence, crucial for Low-Altitude Economy applications (e.g., UAV safety).
