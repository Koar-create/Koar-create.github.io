This research idea extends the WRF–OpenFOAM coupling work toward an operational decision-support layer for urban drone logistics under adverse weather.

## Core hypothesis

High-fidelity wind and turbulence fields at building-resolving scales can expand go/no-go windows beyond coarse mesoscale thresholds, if coupled with robust path-planning under uncertainty.

## Open questions

- How to propagate WRF–CFD uncertainty into route risk scores?
- What policy metrics (delay cost, reroute frequency) matter most to operators?
- Can a lightweight twin run fast enough for same-day operational use?

## Next steps

- Prototype a single-corridor CFD case with gust metrics exported to a planner API.
- Interview operators on acceptable risk thresholds.
